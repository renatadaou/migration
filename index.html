It has been almost a decade since the height of the Syrian Refugee Crisis in 2015. Yet its reverberations persist. With over 5 million registered Syrian refugees globally, its impact remains evident. This crisis has left an indelible mark on popular culture, inspiring numerous movies and books that shed light on its human stories. More importantly, the crisis has been a focal point in news coverage, exposing instances of racism towards Syrians and prompting discussions on immigration policies. The aftermath continues to be felt in Europe and beyond, with ongoing legal battles highlighting the enduring repercussions of this humanitarian crisis.
